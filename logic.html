How the Calculator Works Version 1.0

This calculator uses a monthly compounding engine to project savings and retirement balances over time, accounting for tax, inflation, and withdrawals.

The Annual Calculation Sequence

  • 1. Opening Balance: The year begins with the Start balance. For Year 1, this is your Principal. For subsequent years, it is the Inflation Adjusted End balance from the year prior.
  • 2. Withdrawal Growth: If withdrawals are active, the monthly amount is increased annually to account for inflation.
    Formula: Base Withdrawal × (1 + Inflation Rate) ^ (Year - 1).
    Note: This growth is applied from the first year to maintain consistent purchasing power, even if the start year for withdrawals is set in the future.
  • 3. Monthly Compounding Loop (12 Times): Within each month:
    • Withdrawal First: The monthly withdrawal is deducted from the balance before interest is calculated.
    • Interest Second: The remaining balance earns interest based on the Monthly Rate.
  • 4. Tax Settlement: At the end of month 12, the Total Annual Interest is calculated.
    • SMSF: A flat 15% tax is applied (unless the Pension Phase year has been reached).
    • Personal: Interest is processed through the 2024-25 Stage 3 Tax Brackets (+ optional 2% Medicare Levy).
  • 5. Inflation Deflation: Finally, the nominal balance is "deflated" to show today's purchasing power.
    Formula: End Balance / (1 + Inflation Rate). This adjusted figure becomes the starting point for the next year.
