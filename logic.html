How the Calculator Works Version 1.1

This calculator uses a periodic compounding engine to project savings and retirement balances, accounting for tax, inflation, and frequency-adjusted withdrawals.

The Annual Calculation Sequence

  • 1. Opening Balance: Each year starts with the Start balance. For Year 1, this is the Principal. For later years, it is the Inflation Adjusted End balance from the previous year.
  • 2. Withdrawal Growth: Monthly withdrawals are increased annually by the inflation rate:
    Annual Total = (Monthly Withdrawal × 12) × (1 + Inflation Rate) ^ (Year - 1).
  • 3. Compounding Loop (Frequency based): The year is split into periods based on Compounding Frequency (e.g., 4 for Quarterly, 12 for Monthly):
    • Frequency-Adjusted Withdrawal: The Annual Total is divided by the Frequency. This amount is deducted before interest is calculated for that period.
    • Periodic Interest: The Monthly Rate is converted to a periodic rate: Monthly Rate × (12 / Frequency).
  • 4. Tax Settlement: At the end of the final period of the year, total annual interest is taxed. SMSF is a flat 15%; Personal uses 2024-25 Stage 3 Brackets (+ optional 2% Medicare).
  • 5. Inflation Deflation: The nominal end-of-year balance is deflated to show today's purchasing power:
    End Balance / (1 + Inflation Rate).
