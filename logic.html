How the Calculator Works Version 1.4

This calculator uses a periodic compounding engine to project savings and retirement balances, accounting for tax, inflation, frequency-adjusted contributions, and withdrawals.

The Annual Calculation Sequence

  • 1. Opening Balance: Each year starts with the Start balance. For Year 1, this is the Principal. For later years, it is the Inflation Adjusted End balance from the previous year.
  • 2. Withdrawal Growth: Monthly withdrawals are increased annually by the inflation rate:
    Annual Total = (Monthly Withdrawal × 12) × (1 + Inflation Rate) ^ (Year - 1).
    Note: This growth is applied from the first year even if the start year for withdrawals is in the future.
  • 3. Compounding Loop (Frequency based): The year is split into periods based on Compounding Frequency. Each period executes in this order:
    • Contribution: The monthly contribution (scaled to the period: Monthly Contribution × 12 / Frequency) is added to the balance first. Contributions are applied every period until the Stop Contributing at Year threshold is reached (0 = never stop).
    • Frequency-Adjusted Withdrawal: The inflation-adjusted annual withdrawal total is divided by the Frequency and deducted next.
    • Periodic Interest: Interest is then earned on the remaining balance. The Monthly Rate is converted to a periodic rate: Monthly Rate × (12 / Frequency).
  • 4. Tax Settlement: At the end of the final period of the year, total annual interest is taxed:
    • SMSF: Flat 15% (unless in Pension Phase).
    • Company: Flat 25% on all annual interest.
    • Personal: 2024-25 Stage 3 Brackets (+ optional 2% Medicare).
  • 5. Inflation Deflation: The nominal end-of-year balance is deflated to show today's purchasing power:
    End Balance / (1 + Inflation Rate).
